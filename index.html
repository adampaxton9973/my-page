My thesis

Here is my DPhil thesis on timelike maximal surfaces (TMSs) in Minkowski space. Chapters 1 is an introduction to the field, Chapters 2 through 4 are results on the initial-value problem for TMSs (an extended version of my paper above), and Chapters 5 & 6 contains novel work on the initial-boundary value problem for TMSs. In Chapter 7 I discuss some interesting open problems in the field.

Some notes that I have written

Here are a few reports that I have written during my graduate studies. Now of course I see all kinds of ways they could have been written better. Nonetheless I'm putting them up online in case they may be of use to someone :) I also think it's nice to put these up as they tell a bit of a story about my development as a graduate student.

Scattering and blow-up for semi-linear wave equations : I wrote these notes in summer 2016, under the supervision of Luc Nguyen, whilst trying to learn about wave equations. Its mostly a compilation of quite well-known results on scattering vs blow-up for semi-linear wave equations.

Compactness results for flowing to a harmonic map : I wrote this document on harmonic maps in spring 2016, under the supervision of Melanie Rupflin. It gives a brief introduction to harmonic maps from surfaces, focussing particularly on the phenomenon of "bubbling". There are some pretty pictures to illustrate bubbling, and we also proved a new compactness result for maps from degenerating Riemann surfaces, which is written up in Section 3.

Exotic Spheres : This is my Masters thesis, which was written at UCL in 2014/15, under the supervision of Jonny Evans. The goal was to give an exposition of John Milnor's famous construction of 7 dimensional "exotic spheres".
