My thesis

Here is my DPhil thesis on timelike maximal surfaces (TMSs) in Minkowski space. Chapter 1 is an introduction to the field, Chapters 2-4 are results on the initial-value problem for TMSs, whilst Chapters 5 & 6 contains work on the initial-boundary value problem for TMSs. In Chapter 7 I discuss some interesting open problems in the field.

Notes

Here are a few reports that I wrote during my graduate studies. I'm putting them up online in case they may be of use to someone :)

Scattering and blow-up for semi-linear wave equations : I wrote these notes in summer 2016, under the supervision of Luc Nguyen, whilst trying to learn about wave equations. Its mostly a compilation of quite well-known results on scattering vs finite-time singularity for semi-linear wave equations, but the presentation is unique and a few proofs & heuristics are my own. I hope it should serve as a good survey for anyone interested in the topic :)

Compactness results for flowing to a harmonic map : I wrote this document on harmonic maps in spring 2016, under the supervision of Melanie Rupflin. It gives a brief introduction to harmonic maps from surfaces, focussing particularly on the phenomenon of "bubbling". There are some pretty pictures to illustrate bubbling, and we also proved a new compactness result for maps from degenerating Riemann surfaces, which is written up in Section 3.

Exotic Spheres : This is my Masters thesis, which was written at UCL in 2014/15, under the supervision of Jonny Evans. The goal was to give an exposition of John Milnor's famous construction of 7 dimensional "exotic spheres" (given in Section 4), and along the way we came up across a plenitude of wonderful topics in differential topology such as fibre bundles, de Rham cohomology, cobordism, and characteristic classes.
